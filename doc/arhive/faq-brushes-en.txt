4) How should brushes be cared for?
Answer:
Care for high-end brushes like CTR divides into two types: express cleaning between clients or looks, and deep washing. Since CTR offers both delicate natural hair (luxury squirrel W0667, premium goat W0188) and advanced synthetic taklon (W0644, W0647), the approach must be conscious.
1. Express cleaning (daily maintenance):
- Special brush wipes (alcohol-free or low-alcohol): best for natural hair (squirrel, goat). Gently stroke the brush across a textured wipe and the pigment dissolves; the hair dries in 2–3 minutes.
- Professional cleaning liquids: apply a small amount to a cotton pad or paper towel and swirl the brush in circles.
Important taboo: Do not use harsh medical alcohol antiseptics on natural hair.
Alcohol instantly dries the natural hair cuticle, making it brittle and stiff. For taklon and synthetics, alcohol-based express cleaners are safe.
2. Deep wash (every 1–2 weeks):
- For natural hair (squirrel, goat, sable): use only mild bar soap mousse, professional brush shampoo, or a sulfate-free moisturizing shampoo (preferably labeled gentle or baby). Once a month after washing, you can apply a drop of regular hair conditioner to natural hair for 1 minute and rinse — this restores silkiness.
3. Key drying rules (gold standard):
- Only horizontally or with the tip down: never dry brushes vertically with the hair up. Even though CTR has a triple fixation system and water-resistant 20-year-old wood handles, water running into the ferrule can eventually soften even the strongest glue.
- Keep away from heat: Dry brushes on a towel with the hairs hanging off the edge of a table at room temperature. No radiators or hair dryers!

5) How to wash brushes correctly?
Answer:
Washing premium brushes is not just cleaning off makeup; it is a delicate spa treatment for the hair that must be done by strict rules. Any mistake (wrong water temperature, wrong drying direction) can permanently damage the bundle structure.
CTR gold-standard wash routine:
1. Preparation and cleaner choice:
- For delicate natural hair (luxury squirrel W0667, premium goat W0188) or soft multifunctional models like W0184, use special brush bar soap or mild baby/sulfate-free shampoo.
- For dense synthetic brushes that work with creamy products, first use a drop of oil-based remover to dissolve pigment, then wash it away with soap.
2. Hair direction — strictly down:
- Use cool or barely warm water (hot water destroys the glue base).
- Hold the brush with the hair pointing down under the stream. Water must never flow into the metal ferrule, even though CTR has a triple bundle fixation system.
3. Gentle foaming:
- Apply the cleanser to your palm or a silicone mat.
- Use soft circular or sweeping motions across the palm until foam forms. Do not press hard, squeeze the hair into a fist, or rub it chaotically. That breaks hairs at the base and damages the internal silicone cushion.
4. Rinse the foam thoroughly:
- Rinse under running water with the hair still pointing down until the water runs clear.
5. Dry and reshape properly:
- Gently blot the hair with a soft towel or tissue, squeezing from the base to the tip. Do not twist the brush.
- Use your fingers to restore the original shape (fan, round, slanted). For fluffy natural brushes, you can place a brush guard to keep the hair from splaying while it dries.
6. Dry in the correct position:
- Lay brushes flat on a dry towel. Ideally fold the towel into a small roll and rest the handle on it so the hair points downward off the edge of a table. Air should circulate around the hair.
- Taboo: never dry brushes vertically with the hair up or place them on a radiator.

22) Is duofiber suitable for dense foundation?

Duofiber in professional makeup is often misunderstood. Because it looks airy and lightweight, people assume it is only for sheer textures.

What duofiber is: structure and mechanical design
Duofiber’s secret is its two-level bundle. Unlike classic brushes, it combines two very different fibers:
1. Bottom layer (base) — dense and sturdy: often made from resilient animal hair (premium goat or Italian raccoon) or a dark dense synthetic. It holds the brush shape and prevents collapse under pressure.
2. Top layer (tips) — sparse and elongated: consists of ultrafine synthetic fibers (premium taklon or nylon). These white tips extend above the base by several millimeters.

When you touch the skin with duofiber, only the very tips make contact. They pick up a minimal, precisely dosed amount of product and distribute it. The base provides the necessary resistance.
This changes the dense foundation result:
- Airbrush effect: the long polymer tips break the product into microscopic droplets. With circular motion, the brush basically sprays the foundation across the face. You get full coverage without heaviness.
- Fine layering: for problem areas, duofiber lets you build coverage locally layer by layer. The edges blend seamlessly, so there is no cakey buildup.
CTR duofiber synergy:
- The top layer uses premium taklon that does not absorb dense cream inside the fiber. Product is fully released onto the skin and the brush washes easily, even from long-wear foundations.
- The bottom layer of goat or resilient taklon gives perfect cushion.
- Hand-set assembly preserves the thin end geometry. The brush glides like silk, does not streak, and does not leave lines like cut-budget duofibers.
